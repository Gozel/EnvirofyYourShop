How Envirofy works

Envirofy is the first eco-friendly web browser extension for online shoppers. The goal of our research is to determine which behaviour change techniques are the most effective in supporting online shoppers to buy eco-friendly food products.

Envirofy’s main components are (Figure 1, example on the Tesco website) :

  • Traffic light colour icons: under each food product indicating its environmental impact overall and in relation to production, transportation, and packaging. These icons support knowledge and can be compared across all food products.
  • Reordered display of the food items: according to the total environmental impact, items with small CO2 values are presented at the top of the page (green globes), followed by products with increasingly larger impacts lower on the page (orange or red globes). Restructuring the decision environmentin this way helps users to view eco-friendly options as more convenient, normal, and attractive.
  • Real time personalised feedback: on the overall impact of the items currently in a user’s basket. This is displayed as CO2 and the number of trees that would need to be planted.

Check out this quick demo video to learn how Envirofy applies behaviour intervention technique to help you shop environmentally friendly.
